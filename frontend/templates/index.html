Glossary of Terms

Gross Savings
Raw pharmacy cost difference between brand and generic, based on NADAC unit pricing × fill quantity. Pre-risk.
Risk-Adjusted Savings
Gross savings minus expected switch-failure cost and adherence penalty. Use this number for prioritization.
Generic Equivalent
Same ingredient, strength, form and route — FDA TE code AB. Generally low clinical risk to switch.
Therapeutic Alternative
Different molecule, same drug class. Requires clinical review before switching.
Recommend
Positive risk-adjusted savings, low clinical and access risk. Suitable for proactive outreach.
Review
Positive savings but meaningful clinical or access uncertainty. Requires pharmacist sign-off.
Do Not Switch
Negative risk-adjusted savings or high safety/access flags. Switch blocked.
Clinical Risk Score
0–1 probability of adverse medical event if switch occurs. Derived from failure history, diagnosis, and adherence.
Access Risk Score
0–1 likelihood member cannot obtain the alternative. Based on pharmacy access and zip-level data.
TE Code
FDA Orange Book rating. AB = bioequivalent and substitutable. BX/B* = uncertain.
NADAC
National Average Drug Acquisition Cost — CMS benchmark for retail pharmacy drug costs.
Adherence Score
0–1 measure of on-time refill consistency. Lower scores increase the adherence risk penalty.
